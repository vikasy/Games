Poker Reference

Best to worst

  1. Royal FlushA K Q J 10, same suit
  2. Straight Flush5 consecutive cards, same suit
  3. Four of a Kind4 cards of the same rank
  4. Full House3 of a kind + a pair
  5. Flush5 cards of the same suit
  6. Straight5 consecutive cards, any suit
  7. Three of a Kind3 cards of the same rank
  8. Two Pair2 different pairs
  9. One Pair2 cards of the same rank
  10. High CardNo combination — highest card plays

Tie-breaking order (after a hand matches)

  • High Card: Compare the highest card, then the next-highest card, all the way to the 5th.
  • One Pair: Highest pair wins. If equal, compare kickers from highest to lowest.
  • Two Pair: Compare the top pair, then the lower pair, then the kicker.
  • Three of a Kind: Highest trips win; if tied, compare kickers.
  • Straight / Straight Flush: Higher top card wins. Identical sequences split.
  • Flush: Compare highest card, then 2nd, 3rd, etc. until someone wins.
  • Full House: First compare the three-of-a-kind rank, then the pair.
  • Four of a Kind: Higher quads win. If tied, compare the kicker.
  • Royal Flush: Always a tied hand — split the pot.
