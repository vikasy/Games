Hand History

Welcome to Texas Hold'em! Here's how to play:

  1. Pick your difficulty and press Deal.
  2. You get 2 secret cards. Decide to check, call, raise, or fold.
  3. Watch the shared cards (flop, turn, river) appear on the table.
  4. Make the best 5-card hand using your 2 cards + the shared cards.
  5. If you have the strongest hand (or everyone else folds), you win the pot!

What happens when two players have the same hand?

  • High Card: Whoever has the highest card wins. If those match, compare the next card, and so on.
  • One Pair: The bigger pair wins. If the pairs are the same, the highest extra card (kicker) decides.
  • Two Pair: Compare the bigger pair first, then the smaller pair, then the leftover card.
  • Three of a Kind: The higher set of three wins. If tied, compare the extra cards.
  • Straight / Straight Flush: The one with the higher top card wins. If identical, it's a tie!
  • Flush: Compare cards from highest to lowest until someone has a bigger one.
  • Full House: First compare the three-of-a-kind, then compare the pair.
  • Four of a Kind: The higher four-of-a-kind wins. If the same, the extra card decides.
  • Royal Flush: It's always a perfect tie â€” split the pot evenly!

Words you'll hear at the table

  • Blinds: Small bets that two players must put in before cards are dealt, so there's always something to play for.
  • Hole Cards: Your two secret cards that only you can see!
  • Flop: The first three shared cards flipped onto the table.
  • Turn: The fourth shared card.
  • River: The fifth and final shared card.
  • Check: Skip your turn without betting â€” free! Only works if nobody has bet yet.
  • Call: Put in the same amount as the last bet to stay in the hand.
  • Raise: Bet even more than the last person â€” makes everyone else decide!
  • Fold: Give up your cards and sit this hand out. You lose what you already put in, but save the rest.
  • All-in: Push all your chips in! Go big or go home.
  • Pot: All the chips everyone has bet this hand â€” the winner takes them!
  • Side Pot: An extra pile of chips that forms when someone goes all-in and others keep betting.
  • Kicker: Your highest extra card that breaks a tie when hands are equal.
  • Showdown: When betting is done, everyone shows their cards to see who wins!

Fun tips to play smarter

  • Big hand? Raise! When you have a strong hand, raise to win more chips from your opponents.
  • Not sure? Check! If nobody has bet yet and you're not sure, checking is free â€” it costs nothing.
  • It's OK to fold: Folding a bad hand saves your chips for a better one. Smart players fold a lot!
  • Watch the table: Look at the shared cards â€” could someone else have a flush or a straight?
  • Use "My Hand": Tap the My Hand button to see how strong your cards are and get advice!
  • Stay patient: You don't need to win every hand. Wait for good cards and strike!

Learn cool math while you play!

  • The deck: 52 cards = 4 suits × 13 cards per suit. That means 13 hearts, 13 spades, 13 clubs, 13 diamonds.
  • Outs: Cards left that can help you. If you have 4 hearts, there are 13 − 4 = 9 hearts left in the deck!
  • Chances: Multiply your outs by 2 to estimate your % chance on the next card. 9 outs × 2 = about 18%.
  • Fractions: 9 helpful cards out of 47 unseen cards = 9/47 â€” that's a little less than 1 out of 5.
  • Pot math: If the pot has $100 and you need $20 to call, you're risking $20 to win $100. That's 5-to-1 â€” a good deal if your chances are better than 1 in 5!
  • Combinations: From 7 cards (2 in hand + 5 shared), the game picks the best 5. That's 21 different combinations to check â€” the computer does it for you!
  • Probability: A pair happens about 42% of the time. A flush? Only about 3%. A Royal Flush? About 1 in 650,000 hands!
