Hand History

Welcome to Texas Hold'em! Here's how to play:

  1. Pick your difficulty and press Deal.
  2. You get 2 secret cards. Decide to check, call, raise, or fold.
  3. Watch the shared cards (flop, turn, river) appear on the table.
  4. Make the best 5-card hand using your 2 cards + the shared cards.
  5. If you have the strongest hand (or everyone else folds), you win the pot!

How split pots are decided

  • High Card: Compare the highest card, then the next-highest card, all the way to the 5th.
  • One Pair: Highest pair wins. If equal, compare kickers from highest to lowest.
  • Two Pair: Compare the top pair, then the lower pair, then the kicker.
  • Three of a Kind: Highest trips win; if tied, compare kickers.
  • Straight / Straight Flush: Higher top card wins. Identical sequences split.
  • Flush: Compare highest card, then 2nd, 3rd, etc. until someone wins.
  • Full House: First compare the three-of-a-kind rank, then the pair.
  • Four of a Kind: Higher quads win. If tied, compare the kicker.
  • Royal Flush: Always a tied hand â€” split the pot.

Key lingo at the table

  • Blinds: Forced bets (Small Blind / Big Blind) posted before cards are dealt.
  • Hole Cards: The two private cards dealt to each player.
  • Flop: The first three community cards revealed together.
  • Turn: The fourth community card (also called Fourth Street).
  • River: The fifth and final community card.
  • Check: Pass the action without betting (only if no bet is pending).
  • Call: Match the current bet to stay in the hand.
  • Raise: Increase the current bet amount.
  • Fold: Give up your cards and sit out the rest of the hand.
  • All-in: Betting all your remaining chips.
  • Pot: Total chips wagered that hand; may split into side pots if players are all-in.
  • Side Pot: Extra pot created when one or more players are all-in and others continue betting.
  • Kicker: Unpaired high card used to break ties.
  • Showdown: Revealing remaining hands when betting ends to decide the winner(s).

Quick odds & math tips

  • Rule of 2 & 4: Multiply your outs by 2 (turn) or 4 (flop) to estimate draw odds.
  • Pot Odds: Call if your win % > chips-to-call / (pot + chips-to-call).
  • Outs: Cards left in the deck that complete your hand. 9 outs â‰ˆ flush draw.
  • Equity: Your share of the pot if all cards were dealt out (e.g., 60% equity = expect 60% of the pot).
  • Implied Odds: Factor in chips you expect to win later when draws hit.
  • Bluff Frequency: In theory, bluff roughly 1/3 of the time heads-up when you bet pot-sized.
